# Future — direction ideas

The pre-backlog. Ideas too big or too early to queue, written down so a
future session can pick one up and spec it. Nothing here is a
commitment or in any order; when one graduates, it gets a `<NAME>_SPEC.md`
per CLAUDE.md's multi-wave protocol and its actionable bits move to
`BACKLOG.md`. History stays in `ROADMAP.md`.

## What the game is (test every idea against this)

A 1:100 geographically real Texas where the core pleasure is **roaming
and recognizing** — "this is really where that is." The map is the
thesis. An idea earns its place by making the map *more Texan* or by
giving the player a better way to meet it; ideally it feeds all three
modes (DRIVE/FLY/WALK). Constraints that are identity, not debt: no
build step, deterministic seeded world, parody brand register,
affectionate family-friendly tone — nothing in the world is ever
hostile (the horn *scares*, haunts *drift away*).

## New verbs — things the player does

- **Horse riding (RIDE mode)** — the big one. A fourth mode between
  WALK and DRIVE: slower than the truck, goes anywhere, feels like
  Texas. Buy at a ranch stable (shop idiom); the agriculture track
  builds the world it belongs in, and Haunted Texas wave 2's El Muerto
  builds horse locomotion anyway. Feeds: new mode entirely.
- **Photo mode** — a camera that turns the critter/legend logs into an
  album: aim, frame, snap; species/landmarks/haunts photographed at the
  right moment (bat emergence, wisp night) earn album pages. Cheap to
  verify (aim math, not pixels). Feeds all three modes; multiplies the
  value of every animal and event already shipped.
- **River tubing + lake boats** — the rivers and lakes are real data
  already; the Guadalupe/Comal float is a Texas rite. A slow water mode
  (tube) + a bass boat (maritime idiom, player-driven). Feeds: new
  water verb; makes rivers destinations instead of scenery.
- **Fishing** — small verb, walk-mode, at stock tanks/lakes/coast;
  seeded species table by water body (bass, catfish, redfish), log
  page like the critter log. Could ride along with the boat idea.

## World texture — the map, more Texan

- **Seasons + calendar** — a game-year: **bluebonnet spring** along the
  highway verges (the single most iconic missing texture), summer haze,
  autumn northers, rare ice days. Drives everything below; sky.js
  KEYS/ATMOS are built for it. Feeds all modes.
- **Friday night lights** — autumn Friday nights, small-town stadiums
  glow on the horizon + distant crowd/band audio. One prop + one audio
  bed; enormous soul-per-poly. (Stadium lights are the one sanctioned
  exception to ask sky.js for — beacon/brand-light precedent.)
- **BBQ joints + dance halls** — parody roadside institutions (brands.js
  precedent): a smoker plume visible for miles marks the BBQ joint; a
  Gruene-Hall-style dance hall spills warm light and synth two-step
  after dark. Smell is out of scope; smoke isn't.
- **Radio stations** — regional synth stations on the truck radio
  (country, tejano, a border blaster with a huge range from the Rio
  Grande, a classical hour) — audio.js already synthesizes everything;
  region picks the dial. Drive-mode texture that costs no geometry.
- **Roadside historical markers** — Texas has thousands of the real
  things; a curated hundred as micro-plaques (existing plaque
  machinery) would out-Texas any single landmark. Grep-scale content
  work, perfect register for a Fable content wave.

## Events — the sky and the calendar as actors

- **Storm chasing** — sky.js is already the game's best actor; give
  spring Panhandle storms a rare **tornado** (photographable from a
  respectful distance — the UFO-standoff state machine, inverted).
  Danger reads as spectacle, not damage — nothing breaks, ever.
- **State Fair (fall, Dallas)** — Big Tex is a landmark begging to
  exist; a seasonal fairground (Ferris wheel, corny-dog stand parody)
  on the real Fair Park site, one month of the game year.
- **Rodeo season** — arena + crowd glow + stock pens in Fort Worth /
  Houston / San Antonio on their real calendar weeks; ties into the
  agriculture track's animal props.
- **Meteor showers + launches** — the sky data is real; Perseids
  nights, and a rare **Boca Chica launch** visible from half the coast
  (Starbase pad as a landmark, launch as a scheduled sky event —
  trains/UFO scheduling idioms). The launch is the fireworks-show
  version of the thesis: it really happens *there*.

## Moonshots — someday, maybe

- **Rideable train** — board a freight consist (trains.js already runs
  them on real rails) and watch Texas go by; the game's slow-TV mode.
- **Big Bend done properly** — the park deserves better than terrain:
  Santa Elena Canyon walls, the Window, a Terlingua ghost-town pass;
  the one region where hand-authored terrain drama might be worth it.
- **Border river texture** — international bridges, twin-city glow
  across the Rio Grande at night (El Paso/Juárez is already half the
  skyline), Boquillas crossing rowboat. Texture and affection, never
  politics.
- **A game-year almanac** — if seasons + events land, a HUD almanac
  page ("Bluebonnets peak this week; Fair opens Friday") that turns the
  calendar into a reason to keep a save alive.

## Probably never — and why

- **Hunting** — the horn exists to scare animals *safely*; the log
  exists to admire them. Wrong register, no matter how Texan.
- **Multiplayer** — needs servers and a build pipeline; fights the
  no-build-step, single-file soul. (An *async* echo — seeing a friend's
  truck trail baked into a shared save file — is the only crack in this
  door.)
- **Damage/combat of any kind** — nothing in this world is hostile;
  that's load-bearing tone, not a missing feature.
- **Licensed real brands** — the parody register (Bucky's, H-E-Buddy)
  *is* the voice; real logos would flatten it.
- **True-scale realism** — the mini-world contract (truck ≈ 400 m) is
  what makes the whole state roamable in an evening. Ideas that need
  real scale need a different game.
